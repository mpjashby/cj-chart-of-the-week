Drug use was falling, but that seems to be changing

Self-reported drug use has fallen for most of the past 20 years, but in the past six years the use of cannabis, cocaine and ecstasy has increased, particularly among people aged 16–24. For women, attackers are more likely to have a familiar face

Men of all ages are more likely to be attacked by strangers than by people they know. Women have a lower risk of being victims of violence than men do, but (except before age 25) are as likely to be attacked by an acquaintance as by a stranger.
